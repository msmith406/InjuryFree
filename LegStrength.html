Leg and Hip Strengthening

Why is this important?
Uh, well... strong legs are pretty essential for rowing. Being dilligent about evenly strengthening your lower body will allow you to drive with confidence and power. The last thing you want is to be hesitant because you're hurting or afraid to get hurt.

How do leg injuries happen?
There are lots of ways you can injure your legs and hips. Hip flexors are very easy to injure if you have inproper technique in a boat or on an erg. Make sure that you aren't over-engaging you hip flexors as you pull yourslef up from the finish. This can lead to overuse injuries. Instead, make sure you're activated your core every stroke. Hamstrings are also very susceptible to injury. They're doing a lot of work and can get pretty tired out, especially on longer pieces. Make sure you're stretching and rolling out your legs with a foam roller after practices to avoid straining or pulling a hamstring.

 Supine Straight Leg Hip Flexion

- Lie on your back with one knee bent and the affected leg completely straight.
- Roll leg out so that the toes are pointing to the side
- Bring the toes of the affected leg up towards the knee and contract the muscles on the front of the thigh,   making the leg as straight as possible.
- Lift your leg off the floor or table to 45 degrees keeping it straight.
- Re-contract the muscles on the front of the thigh emphasizing the straight leg.
- Slowly lower the leg to starting position and repeat.

Repeat: 3 sets of 10-15 reps

 Side Lying Hip Abduction

- Lying on the side of the non-involved leg, bend bottom leg and keep top leg straight.
- Bring heel slightly behind mid-line of body.
- Tighten buttocks and lift leg approx. 12-16 inches from the floor.
- Hold and return leg to starting position.
- Keep hips perpendicular to the floor.

Repeat: 3 sets of 10-15 reps

 Side Lying AROM Adduction

- Lie on your side with the bottom leg straight and the top leg bent in front with your foot flat on the floor.
- Keeping your bottom leg straight, contract the muscles of the inner thigh to lift it off of the floor.
- Slowly return to initial position
- Repeat as prescribed.

Repeat: 3 sets of 10-15 reps

 Prone Extension/ Straight Leg raise

- Lie on your stomach with your back in neutral position and your hands under your hips.
- Activate your lower abdominals by drawing in your belly button inward toward your spine.
- Maintain steady breathing while you tighten your buttocks and lift one leg up off of the floor approximately six inches
- Keep your leg straight and hips in contact with the floor.
- Return slowly to initial position and keeping your abdominals activated, repeat with other leg.
- Continue alternating legs.

Repeat: 3 sets of 10-15 reps
