Abs and Lower Back Strengthening

Why is this important?
Keeping your core strong is the key to keeping the rest of your body strong! Plus, who doesn't want abs?

How do lower back injuries happen?
Back injuries are extremely common in rowing and they're notoriously difficult to heal from. Lots of back injuries are caused by improper technique, like pulling with your back instead of driving with your legs. Avoid this, and overuse injuries by stengthening your lower back and core.

How do abdominal injuries happen?
These are less common, but you can definitely strain abdominal muscles by over training them. Make sure you have a rest day in between intense ab workout days.

 Prone Forward Plank on Elbows

- Lie on your stomach with elbows on the ground at 90 degrees.
- Push up from elbows into a plank position.
- Keep your back straight and abdominals drawn in.
- Maintain the position without arching/sagging the lower back and relax.
- Return to start position and repeat.

Repeat: 1 set of 10 reps, hold 10-20sec, daily

 Bird Dog

- Get on your hands and knees with your knees directly under your hips and your hands directly under your   shoulders.
- Your back is in neutral position (slightly arched) and your chin must be slightly tucked in.
- Lift one arm overhead and the opposite leg straight back.
- Return the arm and leg down without touching the ground and touch the elbow to the opposite knee keeping   your back in neutral position and your chin tucked-in.
- Repeat with the other arm and opposite leg.

Repeat: 1 set of 10 reps, hold 10sec, daily

 Supine Scissor Kicks

- Lie on your back with your arms placed to the side and your lower back flat against the floor.
- Activate your lower abdominals by drawing in your belly button.
- Lift your legs about 12 inches off of the floor and spread them apart.
- Cross one leg over the other and return to starting position. Repeat by crossing over with the other leg.
- Do not let your lower back come off the ground.
- Rest and repeat.

Repeat: 3 sets of 15 reps, daily

 Standing Fixed Stance Rotation

- Standing with the feet shoulder width apart and resistance band to one side.
- Keeping the abdominals drawn in/engaged and the bottom of the body stationary.
- Rotate the body to the opposite side of the resistance band.
- Return and repeat.

Repeat: 3 sets of 10-20 reps, daily

 Superman

- Begin lying face down with your arms at your side.
- Tighten the muscles in your buttocks, lower/upper back, and hamstrings.
- Arch your back lifting your upper and lower body toward the ceiling, raising shoulders, chest and thighs off the   floor.
- Maintain a slight chin tuck throughout the exercise.
- Return to starting position, repeat.

Repeat: 2-3 sets of 10-15 reps, daily

 Prone to Hands

- Start position: Lay on your stomach with hands flat on the floor
- Lift your upper body slowly leaving your hips on the floor with your lower body relaxed. As you lift inhale.
- Press up until your elbows are straight.
- Hold this position and exhale.
- Slowly return to the start position.

Repeat: 1 set of 10 reps, daily

 Partial Crunch

- Lie on your back with your knees bent and your feet flat on the floor.
- Tighten your stomach muscles drawing your belly button inward toward your spine.
- Tuck your chin to your chest.
- With your arms outstretched out in front of you, curl your upper body forward until your shoulders clear the   floor, hands reaching just beyond either side of your knees.
- Keep your lower back touching the floor throughout the entire exercise.
- Exhale the air out of your lungs as you lift your body, inhale as you slowly return to starting position.
- Repeat.

Repeat: 2-3 sets of 15 reps, daily

 Abdominal Marching

- Lie on your back with your knees bent and your back in neutral position (slightly arched).
- Draw in your lower abdominals by bringing your belly button inward and slightly pushing your pelvis towards   the ground
- Maintain steady abdominal breathing while you lift one leg up from the hip to 90 degrees while keeping your   knee bent
- Keep your back and pelvis completely still at all times.
- Return slowly to initial position and repeat with other leg.

Repeat: 2-3 sets of 15 reps, daily
